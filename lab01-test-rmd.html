Do some plotting

Finally, here is the plot of the data (message=FALSE avoids informative messages from code):

Height of parents and offsprings

The black dots depict parent-child pairs (although the data claims it is about fathers and sons, Galton was in fact also using mothers and daughters). The red line is one-to-one relationship, meaning that one cm taller parents have in average one cm taller children. As the blue regression line shows, this is not at all the case. Taller parents have taller children, but not as much above the mean as parents. This is what Galton (1886) called regression to mediocrity and we call regression to mean.
